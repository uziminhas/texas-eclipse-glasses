## The Celestial Phenomenon: Solar Eclipse

### What is a Solar Eclipse?

A solar eclipse is a mesmerizing event that occurs when the Moon passes between the Sun and the Earth, casting a shadow on a portion of the Earth's surface. During this celestial alignment, the Moon blocks the direct sunlight, resulting in a temporary darkening of the sky.

There are different types of solar eclipses, including total, partial, and annular eclipses. In a total solar eclipse, the Moon completely covers the Sun, creating a stunning display of the Sun's corona. A partial solar eclipse occurs when only a portion of the Sun is obscured by the Moon. An annular solar eclipse happens when the Moon is at a farther point in its orbit, leading to a ring-like appearance of the Sun.
